              I haven't had the time to test the actual real life battery life for all this combined yet, but just turning off the modem and making the Arduino sleep gave us at least 50% more lifetime. Even though the overall battery life is not great, the percentage gained by some simple changes to the code is actually quite a lot.

                      Christian D. Tuen Charlie • a year ago

                      The 2A current peak is normally only in bursts, and there is a capacitor (orange) on the shield that should handle the current peaks. So you will most likely not experience 2A drawn for long durations of time, but it is still possible that you will. The highest peak we experienced when measuring the total current drawn by the arduino+shield was 380mA, but this will likely vary under different conditions. The power drawn varies with what frequencies are used, and we did some testing on DCS (1800MHz) and E-GSM (900MHz). We found that for DCS the average peak value was about 1W (200mA at 5V), while for E-GSM it was 1.35W (270mA at 5V). Again, your results may wary. The peak values are mostly experienced during connection to the network, and are normally much lower. Amount of sun, time connected to the network, signal strength, frequency, amount of data transmitted, speed of the connection etc. will all impact how long you are able to run your project.
                      Feel free to reply with a link to your project if you want to share your experiences.
